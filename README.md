Here’s the myth that’s costing brands millions: last-click attribution works. It doesn’t. It’s a convenient fiction that assigns credit to the final touchpoint before a sale, ignoring everything that came before. A customer might discover your brand through a TikTok ad, research on Google, and finally convert after clicking a Meta retargeting ad. Last-click gives all the credit to Meta, erasing the influence of the other channels. The result? Brands double down on what looks effective while starving the channels that actually drive discovery.

The problem isn’t just oversimplification—it’s systemic bias. Ad platforms are incentivized to claim as much credit as possible for conversions.
